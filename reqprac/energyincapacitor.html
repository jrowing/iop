Investigation of the Energy Stored in a Capacitor

by Joe Rowing
last updated 1/06/2018

Theory:

The energy stored by a capacitor is given by the equation: $$ U= \frac{1}{2}QV $$ Given that Q = CV then the equation for the energy stored can be written in the form: $$U= \frac{1}{2}CV^{2}$$ . The capacitor can be charged to various values of $V$ and then the energy stored can be determined by using a Joule meter. The energy stored can be measured as the capacitor discharges. A graph of energy stored against $V^2$ should be linear and the value of the capacitance can then be measured.

Apparatus:

  • D.C. power supply
  • Voltmeter (multimeter set on d.c. voltage range or CRO) – resolution ± 0.01 V
  • Joule meter or other mechanism for measuring energy stored
  • Suitable switches
  • Electrolytic capacitors e.g. 1 000 µF or 2 200 µF
  • Resistors e.g. 100 kΩ or other values
