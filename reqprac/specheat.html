Measurement of the Specific Heat Capacity for a Solid

by Joe Rowing
last updated 1/06/2018

Theory:

Assuming no energy losses: Electrical energy supplied by the heater = heat received by the block $$ItV = mc(\theta_{2} – \theta_{1})$$ Where:
$c =$ specific heat capacity and
$\theta_{2} – \theta_{1} = 30$
Hence: $$ C = \frac{ItV}{30m}$$

Apparatus:

  • Thermometer
  • Powersupply
  • Ammeter
  • Stopwatch
  • Metal Block
  • Insulation
  • Voltmeter
  • Heater
  • Top-pan balance

Experimental Method:

The apparatus should be set up as shown.

Use a cylindrical block of the metal to be tested (such as copper or aluminium). The block should be well-lagged using an insulator such as polystyrene and it needs two pre-drilled holes, one for a heater and one for a thermometer. Measure the mass, m, of the block and record its initial temperature, θ1. Switch the heater on and start the stopwatch. Record the voltmeter and ammeter readings. When the temperature has risen by 30 o C switch the heater off and record the time taken, t. The formula can then be used to determine a value for c

Extension

By comparing the specific heat capacity to known constants it is possible to determine the type of metal the block is made from. Remember to quote your sources!

Further guidance
